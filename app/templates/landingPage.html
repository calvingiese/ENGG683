So, it’s your first Canadian Winter

Depending on where you hail from, you may or may not be used to sub-zero temperatures. It is likely though that you haven’t quite seen anything like a Canadian winter before. Knee deep snow, icy wind and -30 degrees Celsius are nothing out of the norm, but if it’s your first time experiencing these conditions you will want to be prepared.

First and foremost, you are going to need to dress appropriately. The longer you anticipate having to be out in the cold, the more layers you will be better off having on. A winter jacket with insulation is an absolute must. It is a good idea when getting one to ensure it is large enough that you can fit a second layer (sweater, hoodie, even another coat) under it as the need may arise.

Next, a good pair of gloves will not only keep your hands warm but help with preventing any potential for frostbite in your fingers. Mittens can be used instead to optimize for warmth as your fingers are all clumped together, but if you will require any dexterity in your hands, it is likely best to stick with gloves. Hand warmers can also be purchased and put into the palms of your gloves for an extra boost on those very cold days.

A ’toque’ as referred to in Canada (also known as a beanie) will keep your ears and head warm and add an element of style to your winterized outfit. These can also be interchanged with a full-on face mask if need be.

The final essential item to have would be a good pair of snow boots. These will keep your feet warm and dry, and many pairs go to mid-shin height which will prevent the boots from being filled with snow and keep the bottom of your pants protected from the wet ground. Make sure they have good grip on the soles as snow is often accompanied with ice, and keep in mind that additional grippers may also be purchased and slipped on to them.

Some additional items that can be added on those extra cold days are scarves, snow pants, long under-wear and thermal socks. Whatever you feel necessary to stay warm. Try to keep some of these items in your car as well even if you don’t feel you need them as a vehicle breakdown in the cold could lead to some chilly wait times. Thanks for reading!
